    Statistics

    • According to recent estimates, over 150 million children worldwide are victims of child labor.
    • Around 10 million children are forced into various forms of slavery, including bonded labor and human trafficking.
    • According to the International Labour Organization (ILO), approximately 1 in 4 victims of modern slavery are children.
    • Girls are disproportionately affected, representing 99% of victims in the commercial sexual exploitation industry.
    • Forced child labor occurs in various industries, including agriculture, manufacturing, domestic work, and mining.
    • The highest prevalence of child labor is found in Africa, where about 1 in 5 children are engaged in child labor.
    • Child labor and slavery are often linked to poverty, lack of access to education, and social inequality.
    • Conflict zones and areas with weak governance are particularly vulnerable to the exploitation of children.

    How to Help

    Ending child slavery requires collective efforts from individuals, governments, and organizations. Here are some ways you can contribute:

    • Support organizations working to combat child slavery through donations and volunteering.
    • Educate others about the issue to raise awareness.
    • Advocate for stricter laws and policies against child labor and slavery.
    • Boycott products and companies that exploit child labor.
